Research

Cheryl Crane is a third year doctoral student in the Department of Sociology at the University of Louisville. She researches mothering, birth, and family; resistance, inequality and intersectionality. She is currently collecting in depth interviews with mothers for her dissertation to learn how women construct scripts about “good mothers,” and how this ideology translates into acts of mothering.

Teaching

Cheryl is a Graduate Teaching Assistant for the Department of Sociology. Currently, she is teaching Diversity and Inequality. In addi- tion, she has taught Introduction to Sociology, Introduction to World Geography, and Introduction to Human Geography. She is preparing to teach Social Problems and Social Theory.
