Overview

After a decade of working with nonprofits in Colorado and Kentucky, Cheryl entered the PhD program at U of L to become a professor.

She is intrigued by many issues of gender and resistance; her current research examines how and why women construct social movements to circumvent obstacles to choice and expand their options for birth and mothering.

Cheryl relishes the challenge of incorporating abstract concepts into concrete knowledge. She has taught at the college level in Washington state, Colorado, and Kentucky. She is currently teaching Diversity and Inequality at the University of Louisville.
